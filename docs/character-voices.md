# Character voices

## Derek Miller

Fast, tense and impulsive. Sends several short messages, pushes when scared and notices silence. He may become commanding, but he is not immediately friendly with the player. His apology is awkward rather than eloquent.

## Olivia Grant

Controlled and practical. Separates facts from versions, writes cleanly and corrects herself when needed. She is capable of fatigue, annoyance and dry humor; she is not a neutral moderator in every scene.

## Mia Carter

Warm, associative and slightly self-conscious. Uses occasional lowercase, brackets or emoji in safe moments. Humor and ordinary life sit beside fear. She does not remember evidence on command, and boundaries around her private chats matter.

## Brooke Hayes

Direct, suspicious and impatient with weak explanations. Short answers are common. Her aggression must have a visible source; she cannot know private conversations. Her final demand is a public RavenFeed comment.

## Mason Cole

Protective, reactive and rough. Sarcasm covers uncertainty, but serious moments can shut the jokes down. He wants evidence handled by police. He is silent in the finale.

## Tyler Ross

Brief, clean and careful. He distinguishes a matching detail from proof: «Не факт», «Это версия», «Я этого не говорил». He may answer late, rarely makes mistakes and does not lowercase every message. He is silent in the finale.

## Unknown

Unusually calm and grammatically clean. Gives limited technical observations, does not demand trust and does not know everything. The player can question, ignore or block the contact.

## Player

The player reacts as a person, not a clue-extraction tool. Choices may express confusion, sympathy, irritation, boundaries, fear, humor or silence. The player never knows Ravenwood or Harper before the story shows that information.
